Who we are

The Club has about 150 members and is affliated to Swim Ireland. It was based at Glenalbyn Pool in Stillorgan, Co Dublin but the pool has now closed for redevelopment. In the meantime the Club remains fully operational and has regular training sessions at Blackrock College Pool.

The Club is a Masters Club meaning that it caters for people from 19 up to 90, or beyond, rather than for younger swimmers. (There is a separate Glenalbyn Swimming Club that caters for those under 19.) Our members compete both in galas and sea races. The Club has elite swimmers who travel to European and even World Masters events, some of whom hold Irish masters record times. It also has a long tradition of particiaption in sea races and is consistently near the top of the rankings in the Leinster Open Sea races.

Many members have no ambitions to win medals but are happy to just compete or simply train to keep fit while other members also swim in the sea at Sandycove and the Forty Foot through the year.

The Club caters for competent swimmers of all speeds whether fast or slow but who are comfortable swimming out of their depth. New members are always welcome.

Leinster Open Sea Races

The club competes in the Leinster Open Sea league (include link) which starts in the last weekend of May and continues to early September. The league is organised by the LOS committee and races are hosted by the sea swimming clubs of Leinster. There are 2/3 races every weekend during the season and swimmers compete to build up points and qualify for the two major races the Dun Laoghaire Harbour Race in August and the end of season Liffey Swim in September.

Masters Galas

Masters galas take place from September until May at various venues around the country. Our swimmers, of all ages, compete in all distance and strokes, in individual events and team relays. The club committee organises trips away with the highlight being the National Long Course Championship held in Limerick in March. Some of our swimmers compete at British, European and World Masters level also.

Social

The social side of swimming is very important at Glenalbyn Masters, indeed, it is as important as the swimming. We organise swim holidays overseas, social nights out, coffees after swimming and always go for a few drinks after the races.

Pool Training

Our club training session with coach Pawel Rudzinski is in Blackrock College swimming pool every Tuesday from 8-9pm from September May. We take a break during the summer season when our members prefer to opt for the sea. In addition to our weekly club session, many of our members continue daily training at the coached public sessions at Newpark swimming pool. Some also train during public hours at Loughlinstown.

Social Sea Swimming

The highlight of the week for many of our members it the ritual swim at the Forty Foot / Sandycove every weekend. We meet for a social informal swim with swimmers from other clubs at 12 noon every Saturday and Sunday from September May. Sea swimming is not organised by the club and all swimmers swim at their own risk, assessing the conditions and distances for themselves. Some do a dip for a few minutes, others stay in longer. All swimmers acknowledge that the club has no responsibility for their sea swimming.

New Members

If you are a competent swimmer, comfortable swimming out of your depth, you are welcome to join Glenalbyn Masters. We are always delighted to welcome new members and have up to twenty new members joining each year. Swimming lessons are not provided so if you are a total beginner, you will need to get lessons first before joining. Please contact us using the contact us message system on our website and we will get in touch with you directly.
